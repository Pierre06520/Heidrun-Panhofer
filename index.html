About

Dr. Heidrun Panhofer is a leading figure in the field of Dance Movement Therapy (DMT), dedicated to exploring the wisdom of the body as a source of knowledge, healing, and transformation.

Since 2003, she has directed the Master's and Postgraduate Diploma in Dance Movement Therapy at the Department of Clinical and Health Psychology, Autonomous University of Barcelona (UAB), Spain — the first academic programme of its kind in the Spanish-speaking world.

A clinician, educator, and researcher, Heidrun has developed an internationally recognised body of work around embodiment, body memory, and movement-based supervision. She brings together rigorous academic inquiry and deep somatic practice to advance DMT in clinical, educational, and community settings.

She is co-founder and former President of the Spanish Association of Dance Movement Therapy (ADMTE) and serves on the International Advisory Panel of the Routledge journal Body, Movement and Dance in Psychotherapy.

Research

Heidrun's research is grounded in the conviction that embodied knowledge is as legitimate and rigorous as linguistic knowledge. Her work spans clinical, epistemological, and socio-political dimensions of DMT.

◈

Body Memory & Embodiment

Drawing on Fuchs' (2003, 2012) conceptual framework, Heidrun explores how the body stores and re-enacts past experience. Her research clarifies the implications of body memory for clinical practice, dance, and psychotherapy research.

◈

Clinical Supervision

Heidrun has pioneered embodied approaches to psychotherapy supervision, rigorously distinguishing knowledge derived through language from knowledge derived through somatic experience without conscious mediation.

◈

Interculturality & Social Justice

Her work with refugees, migrants, and displaced communities examines how social exclusion shapes the embodied sense of self, and how movement-based practices can restore agency, creativity, and wellbeing.

◈

Movement Observation & Analysis

Rooted in Laban Movement Analysis (LMA), Heidrun investigates the use of movement observation as a research methodology, exploring ways of verbalising embodied experience and yielding meaning in movement.

◈

Metaphor & Movement

Her studies investigate how movement stimulates and enhances metaphorical language, bridging DMT, cognitive linguistics, and embodiment theory to understand non-verbal meaning-making.

◈

Relational Dynamics in DMT

Heidrun explores "Moments of Meeting" — instances of special intersubjective connection between therapist and patient — and their role in shifting implicit relational knowing and promoting lasting change.

Teaching & Practice

Heidrun is committed to the highest standards of DMT education, training therapists who integrate somatic awareness, clinical rigour, and ethical presence.

Master in Dance Movement Therapy — UAB

Since 2003, Heidrun has directed this pioneering programme at the Autonomous University of Barcelona — the first of its kind in the Spanish-speaking world. The programme integrates clinical training, movement practice, and research methodology.

masterdmt.uab.cat →

International Guest Teaching

Heidrun lectures and supervises in DMT training programmes across Europe. Her teaching covers embodied supervision methods, movement analysis, intercultural practice, and DMT in specialist populations including refugees, migrants, and palliative care.

Clinical Supervision

As a senior supervisor and member of the Spanish Association of Dance Movement Therapy (ADMTE), Heidrun offers clinical supervision rooted in embodied practice, helping therapists access and integrate somatic knowledge in their work.

Palliative Care & Specialist Practice

Her clinical writing and practice highlight the unique value of DMT in end-of-life contexts — offering kinaesthetic accompaniment, empathic presence, and non-verbal communication beyond what language can provide.
